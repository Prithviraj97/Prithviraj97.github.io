About

I am currently a Visiting Lecturer in the Department of Engineering & Computer Science at McNeese State University, where I teach programming fundamentals and machine learning. My work bridges theoretical computer vision research with practical applications in robotics and edge computing.

I completed my MS in Computer Science at the University of Louisiana at Lafayette under the co-advisement of Dr. Raju Gottumukkala and Dr. Anthony S. Maida., focusing on real-time object detection and tracking of fast-moving small objects. Prior to that, I earned my BS in Computer Science with a minor in Mathematics from McNeese State University, where I served as President of the ACM chapter and founding member of the robotics club.

Originally from Birgunj, Nepal, I grew up in a working-class family and spent much of my childhood in my ancestral village. After completing secondary education, I came to the United States to pursue higher education and research opportunities. I proud of how far I have come.

Research Interests

My research focuses on advancing 3D computer vision algorithms for robotics applications, particularly in challenging scenarios involving fast-moving small objects. I work at the intersection of classical computer vision, deep learning, and physics-informed models to create robust perception systems that operate efficiently on edge devices.

Primary Areas

  • 3D Computer Vision
  • Object Tracking & Detection
  • Robotics Perception
  • Edge Computing & TinyML
  • Physics-Informed Neural Networks
  • Reinforcement Learning

During my graduate work, I developed novel approaches to tracking rapidly moving small objects in 3D space using RGB-D cameras. This work addressed fundamental challenges in motion blur and localization accuracy that existing algorithms struggle with. I am passionate about making advanced vision algorithms practical and deployable on resource-constrained devices like Jetson TX and Raspberry Pi.

Selected Projects

MS Thesis: Real-time Object Detection and Tracking of Fast-moving Small Objects using RGB-D camera and Computer Vision Technicques.

Developed novel tracking algorithms combining Kalman filtering with kinematic models to detect and track fast-moving small objects in 3D space using RGB-D cameras. Experimented with YOLOv5-8 and various single/multi-stage detectors. Results demonstrated significant improvements in tracking accuracy for challenging scenarios with motion blur.

View Thesis →

Object Detection with Deep Reinforcement Learning

Formulated object detection as a sequential decision-making process where an RL agent refines bounding box predictions. Trained on PASCAL VOC2012 using VGG16 backbone, achieving AP of 36.68 and recall of 59.0 for IoU threshold of 0.5.

Read Paper →

Learning with Limited Samples

Conducted experimental analysis comparing CNNs, Compact Convolutional Transformers, and Vision Transformers on image classification with limited data. Results showed that lighter CNN models significantly outperformed heavier architectures like ViT in both accuracy and speed for small datasets (CIFAR-10, CIFAR-100, MNIST).

Transformer-based NLP and Vision

Implemented transformers for sentiment analysis, language translation (English-French, English-Spanish, Portuguese-English), and image classification. Achieved 78% exact match on question answering and 70% test accuracy on CIFAR-10 with Vision Transformers.

Drillobotics: Automated Drill Rig

Collaborated with the Petroleum Engineering Department to design an automated drill rig prototype. Integrated multiple sensors for real-time precision analysis and championed automation in oil field drilling operations.

Bidirectional Autonomous Robot

Built a bidirectional robot from scratch implementing BFS for optimal path planning. The robot used ultrasonic and lidar sensors for obstacle detection and dynamic path recomputation.
