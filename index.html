Research Interests

My primary research interest-both theory and application- is Computer Vision specially 3D object tracking and detection with application in perception system design for robots. During my time as a graduate student at the UL Lafayette, I was primarily involved in research and implementation of Vision Algorithms for tracking rapidly moving small objects in 3D space. I am also interested on the Optimization of computer vision algorithms to enable smooth operations on small edge devices like JetsonTX, Raspberry Pi. Modern Vision Algorithms are very accurate in detection and tracking of larger slow moving object, however same algorithms fail to properly localize small objects like racquetball since the movement is more erratic leading to motion blur, and various other problems. My ambition is to work on solving the problem of detecting, tracking and trajectory tracking of fast-moving small objects using physics-based motion models, and physics-based deep neural networks.

While at UL Lafayette, I was also working as a Software Engineering Research Associate for Louisiana Transportation Research Center. I helped various Researchers and GeoTech Engineers with implementation of their idea in to usable Software. I am greatly fond of Software Engineering principles and love coding, and learning new tech stack. I love teaching and sharing my knowledge with peers and juniors.

Thesis

MS Thesis: Real-time Object Detection and Tracking of Fast-moving Small Objects using RGB-D camera and Computer Vision Technicques

The thesis explores the use of stereo camera for detection and tracking of fast-moving small objects in 3D space. Several Object Detection and Tracking algorithms were experimented. For Object Detection modern YOLO algorithms like YOLOv5, YOLOv7, YOLOv8 as well other mutli and single stage detectors were used. We implemented Kalman Filter based algorithms for the tracking purpose. After several experiments with the state-of-the-art models, the results were unsatisfactory so I worked on augmenting the use of Kinematics to design new tracking system that can enhance the tracking and trajectory forecasting of fast-moving tiny objects. Undergraduate Capstone Project: Bidirectional autonomous robot for optimal path planning

Me and my friend 'Blake Drost' worked on building small robot from scratch, that could traverse in forward or backward direction to find the optimal path to reach the target. We implemented heuristic search algorithms which the robot used to find the optimal path before making move towards the target. The robot had multiple ultrasonic and small lidar sensors to detect obstacle in the path. If there were obstacle in the optimal path the robot would try to avoid it and if nothing works it will compute new optimal path from the current position and traverse towards that position.
